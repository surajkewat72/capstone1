# Retail Analytics Capstone
## Data-Driven Business Intelligence

**Team / Presenter:** Suraj Kewat  
**Date:** April 2026

---

## The Problem
The retail sector faces significant challenges in optimizing revenue and customer retention.
This project addresses:
- Identifying high-value product categories.
- Understanding seasonal revenue trends.
- Segmenting customers based on spending patterns.
- Providing data-driven recommendations.

---

## Dataset & Methodology
- **Data Source:** Over 9,400 retail transaction records.
- **ETL Process:** 
  - Nulls imputed or removed.
  - Duplicates eliminated.
  - Outliers managed using IQR.
  - Engineered the *Revenue* metric.
- **Tools:** Python (Pandas, Seaborn), Tableau Public.

---

## Key Performance Indicators
1. **Total Revenue:** $15.2M (estimated across categories)
2. **Average Order Value (AOV):** $758
3. **Category Penetration:** 'Books' and 'Clothing' dominate at 60%.
4. **Customer Lifetime Value (LTV) Proxy:** VIP customers average $4,368.

---

## Key Insights
- **Digital Payment Dominance:** Credit Card and PayPal drive over 70% of transactions.
- **Emerging Crypto Market:** 10% of revenue from Crypto, suggesting a niche tech-early demographic.
- **Stable Customer Base:** Top 10 customers contribute only 0.61% of revenue. Low concentration risk.

---

## Business Recommendations
1. **Optimize Payment Gateways:** Renegotiate fees with PayPal and Credit Card processors due to high volume.
2. **VIP Loyalty Program:** Create an automated VIP tier for the high-spend 'power users'.
3. **Niche Marketing:** Capitalize on the strong Crypto presence with 'early-access' drops.
